Non-Profit Organization Site Redesign

For this project we were assigned to evaluate and redesign the user experience and functionality of an aspect of the site of a non-profit organization. We chose the site of YWCA Minneapolis.

Research

To gather data, we conducted a survey along with 5 user interviews and a stakeholder interview. We also built a competitor analysis of other non-profit sites.

The top 3 reasons people donated:

- to make a positive impact

- a personal connection to the cause

- trust in the organization's reputation

The weaknesses of the YWCA site:

- lack of immediate information

- disorienting donation process

- Needs more than just orange for color

From our research, we constructed a User Persona. Sarah Smith chooses to donate to organizations she believes in and trusts can put her donation to good use.

Users like Sarah Smith want to donate to causes they believe in and that will have a strong impact, but can be walled by long, complicated, and / or unclear donation processes.

The site for the non-profit organization YWCA Minneapolis has a page for users to donate money for their cause, but the form suffers from being dense and disorienting.

How might we improve YWCA's donation process so that users who wish to donate can do so easily and efficiently, leading to improved user experience and an increase in donations overall?

We decided we wanted to focus on improving how the user experiences the donation process when visiting the YWCA Minneapolis site.
